Wolfgang - measured objectively

This is a piece I wrestled with whether or not I should write. Right now however I am applying to a few jobs, and I've decided this is something I should write to help a hiring manager.

One of my traits is in-depth research, so I've read into the science and psuedo-science of what Human Resources hiring is likely motivated by and searching for. Now let's do a quick aside. When I said one of my traits is research, did I substantiate that claim? Not really. Perhaps after reading some of my articles you draw that conclusion, but it's still a subjective decision you make.

subjective

based on or influenced by personal feelings, tastes, or opinions.

Now perhaps someone's opinion of my research carries some weight, they're an influential person and most people agree instincively with them. Ethos. Nevertheless that is a subjective opinion. For instance, a PhD candidate might read my articles and not find enough citations to their standard. Their subjective opinion is then that I am not good at research. Both people can be correct, subjectively.

objective

not influenced by personal feelings, interpretations, or prejudice

The strongest descriptions of a person's ability are backed by objective facts. It's not important whether someone claims to be capable, or someone else claims that person is capable. What truly matters is when that person performed in measurable ways only someone capable could have performed.

Therefore in this list you will find some of the ways I have objectively performed over the years. Recall from the beginning this isn't an article I've been rushing to write. Any behavior interpreteted as bragging or otherwise conceitful is perceived as extremely negative. This is targeted to a hiring professional needing a quick objective way to measure my potential success as a new hire. The science supports this method of hire as you can read in my meta-analysis article on predictors of job success.

Tests

ACT Raw Score (Composite): 32 Percentile (2010): 98th

SAT Raw Score (Composite): 2090 Percentile (2010): 96th

ASVAB: Percentile: 98

These are the only tests I can provide percentiles for. I've taken a few other ones for companies and military service which suggest similar percentiles.

Accomplishments

B.S. 160 credit hours completed in 8 semesters (averaging 20 per semester, fulltime load being 12) Technical degree in business operations, second major in foreign language

Work Never fired. Never disciplined.

Criminal record None

Here things could become subjective, so I'm keeping things minimal. There are plenty of other accomplishments I'm proud of, team championships in Chess and Lacrosse for instance, but I'm only listing simple work related personal facts. It's not a unique accomplishment to avoid being fired or criminality, but both are relevant to integrity in the workplace.

Bottom line

Hiring managers are trained to search for objective measures that proxy for intelligence and accomplishment. The research suggests the highest predictor of job training success and job success is intelligence. (Read one paper HERE.)

Key takeaway: I fall into the upper range (top 3%) required for entry into cognitively demanding careers such as law, medicine, engineering or business mathematics. This suggests I can acquire a deeper understanding of a job role in a shorter period of time with corresponding training and job success.

Objectively speaking.
